Compass Accuracy Limitations

The compass readings displayed by PointMe.live are derived from your smartphone's built-in magnetometer sensor. While modern phone magnetometers are capable of good accuracy under ideal conditions, the following factors can significantly affect readings:

  • Device hardware quality: Budget phones typically have lower-quality sensors with larger error margins than premium devices
  • Calibration state: An uncalibrated sensor can produce readings that are off by 20° or more
  • Magnetic interference: Metal objects, electronics, vehicle dashboards, and magnetic accessories near the phone can severely distort readings
  • Physical environment: Buildings with steel reinforcement, underground structures, and certain geological formations can interfere with Earth's local magnetic field
  • Tilt and orientation: Holding the phone at an angle introduces tilt-compensation errors
  • Software implementation: Browser API implementations vary across devices and operating systems

Magnetic vs True North

This tool displays magnetic North, not true geographic North. The difference (magnetic declination) varies by location and can be up to 20° or more in some regions. For uses requiring true North, apply the appropriate declination correction for your location. See our article on magnetic declination.

Not for Safety-Critical Use

PointMe.live is explicitly not suitable for:

  • Aviation navigation
  • Maritime navigation at sea
  • Backcountry wilderness navigation where getting lost could be life-threatening
  • Professional surveying or land measurement
  • Any situation where an incorrect heading could result in injury or harm to yourself or others

For these uses, please employ certified navigation instruments and consult qualified professionals.
